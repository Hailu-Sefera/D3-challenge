Correlations Discovered Between Health Care and Poverty

In this D3 Chart, I have explored the relation between Health care and Poverty. Data was collected from the American Community Survey (ACS) and the Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System (BRFSS) for 2014. Each marker represents an individual state in the U.S. As the chart indicates, states with less poverty seem to have less lack of Health care than states with higher poverty. Mississippi is the state with 21.5% of the population in poverty and has the higher lack of health care percentage of people with 18.8%, wheras states like New Hampshire, Maryland, Connecticut, North Dakota, Hawaii, Minnesota and Massachusetts have poverty rates of less than or equal to 12% and have over 85% of their population have access for healt care.
